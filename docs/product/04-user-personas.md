# 1. Purpose

This document defines the primary user groups for InterviewPilot AI and their goals, challenges, and expectations. Understanding user personas helps guide product decisions, prioritization, and user experience design.

---

# Persona 1 — Computer Science Student

## Profile

- Age: 20–24
- Final-year student
- Applying for internships and graduate software roles

## Goals

- Gain confidence before interviews
- Practice common behavioral questions
- Improve communication skills
- Understand interview expectations

## Pain Points

- Limited real interview experience
- Anxiety during interviews
- Difficulty expressing technical knowledge
- No personalized feedback

## Success Criteria

- Feels confident speaking during interviews
- Learns how to structure answers
- Understands areas for improvement

---

# Persona 2 — Early Career Software Engineer

## Profile

- Experience: 0–3 years
- Preparing for product company interviews

## Goals

- Practice technical discussions
- Improve behavioral responses
- Prepare for role-specific interviews

## Pain Points

- Unsure how interviewers evaluate responses
- Difficulty explaining previous projects
- Limited opportunities for realistic practice

## Success Criteria

- Receives realistic follow-up questions
- Improves technical communication
- Builds interview confidence

---

# Persona 3 — Experienced Professional

## Profile

- Experience: 3–10 years
- Applying for senior software roles

## Goals

- Prepare for leadership interviews
- Improve storytelling
- Practice architecture discussions

## Pain Points

- Rusty interview skills
- Limited mock interview options
- Generic interview preparation platforms

## Success Criteria

- Receives challenging conversations
- Practices decision-making explanations
- Improves executive communication

---

# Persona 4 — Career Switcher

## Profile

- Transitioning into software engineering
- Self-taught or bootcamp graduate

## Goals

- Build interview confidence
- Learn industry expectations
- Practice technical conversations

## Pain Points

- Lack of professional experience
- Unsure how to answer behavioral questions
- Fear of technical interviews

## Success Criteria

- Learns interview flow
- Understands communication gaps
- Gains confidence

---

# Common User Needs

Regardless of experience level, all users expect:

- Natural conversations
- Minimal setup
- Fast feedback
- Actionable improvements
- A polished and intuitive interface
